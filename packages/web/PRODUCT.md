# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Home hobbyist soapmakers and experienced artisan makers, served from the same interface. They arrive mid-craft, planning a batch, and range from nervous beginners who need safe numbers and reassurance to fluent formulators who want speed and control. The interface should meet both: understandable enough for a first bar, deep enough for a practiced one. The job is the same across that range — work out a recipe you can trust enough to actually make, and understand why it behaves the way it does.

## Product Purpose

A soapmaking calculator that turns oils, lye, water, superfat, and additives into a safe, balanced recipe across cold process, hot process, and liquid soap. Beyond the raw numbers, it exists to make formulation legible: to show how each choice shifts the bar's properties — hardness, cleansing, conditioning, and the rest — so the user leaves understanding the recipe, not just holding it. Success is a maker who trusts the output and grasps the reasoning behind it.

## Positioning

The soap calculator you actually understand: modern, calm, and clear where the established tools are cluttered spreadsheets. It shows you why the bar turns out the way it does, not just the lye weight.

## Brand Personality

A calm, precise instrument with warmth. Expert and trustworthy — it plainly knows its chemistry — but approachable, never intimidating. Contemporary and uncluttered, where the confidence comes from clarity rather than decoration. It lives in the family of Linear's restraint and Stripe's talent for making complex things legible, dressed in the warm, natural register of the craft rather than cold tech.

## Anti-references

Not the legacy soap calculators (SoapCalc and kin): dense table-soup, dated, visually noisy. Not the generic AI/SaaS template: gradient hero, identical card grids, tracked-uppercase eyebrows, purple-to-blue everything. Not cutesy or crafty: hand-drawn doodles, bubbles, pastel scrapbook clip-art that undercuts trust. Not a heavy enterprise dashboard: corporate, chart-junk, work-software density.

## Design Principles

Legibility is the product. Every number and warning should be readable and its reasoning visible; when a value needs trust, show the range it sits in.

Calm precision over decoration. Confidence comes from clarity and exactness, not visual flourish — when in doubt, remove.

Expert, not intimidating. Serve the nervous beginner and the fluent pro from one surface; depth is available, never in the way.

Earned familiarity. Use standard, trusted product patterns so the tool disappears into the task instead of inventing novelty for its own sake.

Warmth from the craft. The natural, tactile warmth of soapmaking lives in the palette, tone, and detail — not in gimmicks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor: body text at least 4.5:1 against its background, full keyboard operability with a visible focus state, and a reduced-motion alternative for any motion. Sensible defaults beyond that; no formal AAA target.
